Research

I'm very interested in purposeful collaborations, such as those in open source. It's fascinating to see how such collaborations succeed, despite people participating in them being so different: typically they are a mixture of volunteers and professionals, they come from a mixture of cultural and educational backgrounds, they are interested in, motivated by, and good at different things, and they are geographically distributed all over the world. And while most of the previous work in this area has considered these people to be equal, my work focuses on exactly this diversity: how differences between participants in a single community, or differences between communities, relate to individual-level and community-level outcomes.

I often find myself mining repositories, using metrics, and performing all kinds of statistical analyses. As a hobby I'm also interested in all kinds of computational geometry and algorithmic problems.
